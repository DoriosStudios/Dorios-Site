## What this machine can do

The station is built for long-term equipment progression. If you are investing in tools, weapons, armor, or enchant-heavy utility items, this machine becomes one of the most important support blocks in the entire addon.

- Process multiple items in parallel in the main work area.
- Add or improve enchantments based on installed module strength.
- Remove configured curses during item curation.
- Repair durability damage as part of the same processing cycle.
- Apply reinforcement to extend long-term durability value.
- Convert enchantments into enchanted books.
- Convert enchantments into XP fluid when extraction resources are missing.
- Provide separate feedback for main processing and disenchanting.

## Functional overview

The Enchantment Station is divided into two major workflows. The upper work area is focused on upgrading and maintaining gear, while the lower section handles disenchanting and XP recovery. This split makes the machine useful both when you are building your best equipment and when you are cleaning out old enchanted items.

In practice, the station works best as a dedicated progression room block. One side improves items through enchantment, curation, repair, and reinforcement. The other side makes sure unwanted enchanted gear can still be turned into something valuable instead of being wasted.

---

## Main gear operations

The main work area supports a 3×3 processing field, allowing several items to be worked on at the same time. Each item can follow a different path depending on its durability state, current enchantments, and the modules installed in the machine.

### Enchanting

Enchanting is the machine's most obvious job, but it is also more sophisticated than a simple one-roll system. The station builds a supported enchant result based on item compatibility and module strength, then applies upgrades when a better result is available.

- Adds supported enchantments to eligible gear.
- Improves existing enchantments when the module level allows it.
- Consumes XP fluid based on the amount of enchant changes performed.
- Becomes dramatically stronger as enchantability modules improve.

### Curse curation

Curse curation is one of the reasons this machine feels premium. Instead of only adding power, it can also clean up bad outcomes and remove configured curses from gear that would otherwise be annoying to keep using.

- Removes supported curse effects from eligible items.
- Helps rescue gear that would otherwise feel compromised.
- Can happen alongside enchanting instead of replacing it.
- Benefits from curse protection support for cleaner results.

### Repairing

Repair happens automatically when the machine receives a valid damaged item. That means the station can restore durability while still acting as an enchanting and reinforcement platform, instead of forcing you into a separate maintenance machine.

- Repairs damaged gear during normal processing.
- Works without needing a separate repair-only mode.
- Combines naturally with reinforcement and enchant improvement.

### Reinforcing

Reinforcement is one of the station's most distinct mechanics. Instead of being only a temporary buff, it acts like a durability reserve that strengthens the item and helps preserve its lifespan across repeated use.

- Applies reinforcement to supported durability items.
- Scales final reinforcement strength by reinforcement module tier.
- Improves the long-term value of frequently used tools, armor, and weapons.
- Pairs especially well with high-investment gear.

### Reinforcement values

Reinforcement strength is determined by the installed reinforcement module tier.

- **No reinforcement module**
  - No reinforcement target is applied.

- **Reinforcement Module I**
  - Sets the reinforcement target to **25%** of the item's maximum durability.

- **Reinforcement Module II**
  - Sets the reinforcement target to **50%** of the item's maximum durability.

- **Reinforcement Module III**
  - Sets the reinforcement target to **100%** of the item's maximum durability.

### Reinforcement behavior in practice

Once reinforcement has been applied, the item gains a reserve that can help recover missing durability during use. This makes reinforced equipment feel more forgiving in long sessions and reduces how often prized gear has to be sent back for heavy maintenance.

- Best suited for tools, weapons, and armor.
- Not meaningful for items that do not use durability.
- Most valuable on equipment used constantly in combat or mining.

### Reinforcement modules

Reinforcement modules are more straightforward than enchantability modules, but they completely change how valuable the machine is for long-term gear ownership.

- Control the reinforcement target applied to supported items.
- Raise durability reserve strength by tier.
- Matter most for frequently used combat and mining equipment.

### Curse protection module

Curse protection is not just defensive padding. It helps the station avoid bad outcomes during enchant processing and strengthens curse curation as part of a cleaner refinement loop.

- Reduces the chance of curse-related outcomes.
- Helps maintain cleaner enchant results during repeated processing.
- Supports safer high-value enchanting sessions.

## Disenchanting methods

The lower section of the machine is dedicated to disenchanting. It supports two distinct modes, which makes it useful both for reclaiming individual enchantments and for turning unwanted enchanted items into stored XP value.

### Extract mode

Extract mode is the direct conversion path. It is the best option when you want to preserve the enchantment itself, not just its value.

To operate properly, the station needs:

- one enchanted source item;
- a valid catalyst, normally **Refined Aetherium Shard**;
- regular books for conversion;
- free output space for enchanted books;
- sufficient energy.

When the process completes, the machine:

- creates enchanted books from the extracted enchantments;
- spends catalyst and books according to the amount extracted;
- updates the original item so only the remaining enchantments stay on it.

### Absorb mode

Absorb mode is the fallback path when extraction materials are incomplete. Instead of producing enchanted books, the machine converts the source enchantments into XP fluid and stores that value internally.

This mode is especially useful when you are clearing out surplus enchanted items and want to feed future enchanting work instead of preserving each individual enchantment.

- Activates when the extraction path is incomplete.
- Waits through a short conversion delay before releasing XP value.
- Converts enchantment strength into XP fluid gain.
- Stops early if the XP tank has no free space left.

### Common disenchant blockers

The disenchant section commonly pauses for a few very specific reasons.

- The source item is missing.
- The source item is stacked instead of being alone.
- The item has no valid enchantments to process.
- There is no free output space for book extraction.
- Energy is unavailable.
- The XP tank is full or unavailable for absorb mode.

## UI sections and responsibilities

The two screenshots above show why the station feels denser than most machines in the addon. It is not only a processor; it is also a monitoring surface, with separate feedback for main processing and disenchanting.

### Main status panel

The main status area summarizes what the station is doing in the gear-processing field.

- Shows whether the machine is actively working or waiting.
- Reflects enchanting, repairing, reinforcing, and curation states.
- Helps identify whether an item is already in a finished state.

### Main work area

The main work area is where your tools, armor, and other valid gear pieces are refined.

- Supports parallel item handling.
- Lets different items sit at different stages of progress.
- Rewards batch preparation when upgrading multiple pieces of gear at once.

### Disenchant section

The disenchant section behaves like a dedicated secondary machine built into the same block.

- Handles extraction and absorption separately from the main work area.
- Reads catalyst, books, source item, and output availability.
- Displays mode, state, and missing-resource feedback.

### Output area

The output area is especially important for extract mode because the station needs room to place enchanted books.

- Stores extracted enchanted books.
- Can bottleneck the machine if left full.
- Should be cleared often during large recovery sessions.

### Energy and XP displays

The machine constantly depends on both power and stored XP value when advanced enchanting is involved.

- Energy supports all active work.
- XP fluid supports enchant-related upgrades.
- Shortages in either resource can pause progress.

## Practical operation checklist

1. Decide whether you are improving gear or recovering enchantments.
2. Insert eligible gear into the main work area when focusing on upgrades.
3. Install the modules that match your desired outcome.
4. Maintain both energy and XP fluid for advanced enchant work.
5. For extract mode, provide catalyst, books, and enough free output space.
6. For absorb mode, allow the machine time to convert enchantments into XP fluid.
7. Clear outputs and repeat in batches for the best workflow.

---

## Operational notes

- Cycle duration scales with the amount of active work on each item.
- Energy demand rises with operation complexity and module influence.
- Main processing and disenchanting can run in parallel sections.
- Disenchanting expects a single source item, not stacked inputs.
- If no valid enchantments are present, the disenchant section remains idle.
