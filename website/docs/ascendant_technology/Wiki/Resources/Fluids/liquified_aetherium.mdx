## How to make Liquified Aetherium

Liquified Aetherium is made in the [Liquifier](/docs/ascendant_technology/Wiki/Machines/liquifier).

Ascendant currently provides two native routes:

- **1 Aetherium Ingot → 1000 mB Liquified Aetherium**
- **4 Aetherium Shards → 1000 mB Liquified Aetherium**

The ingot route can also return **Obsidian** as a bonus byproduct, but the shard route is usually the more economical way to fill a tank.

---

## What Liquified Aetherium is used for

### Catalyst Weaver recipes

The [Catalyst Weaver](/docs/ascendant_technology/Wiki/Machines/catalyst_weaver) uses Liquified Aetherium in specialist conversion work.

Its native fluid recipe turns **Crying Obsidian Dust** and other catalysts into **Stabilized Obsidian Dust**, consuming **250 mB** of Liquified Aetherium per craft.

### Duplicator fuel fluid

The [Duplicator](/docs/ascendant_technology/Wiki/Machines/duplicator) is built around **Liquified Aetherium** as its base working fluid.

That means the fluid is not just a one-off recipe ingredient; it is part of the machine's duplication runtime and long-form cloning loops.

### Capsule logistics

Like Ascendant's other advanced fluids, Liquified Aetherium can be packed into capsules for transport and storage.

---

## Practical notes

- If you are liquefying Aetherium specifically for machine use, **4 shards per bucket** is usually the smarter route than melting finished ingots.
- Liquified Aetherium is best treated as a **precision machine fluid**, not a general-purpose industrial liquid.
- It pairs naturally with advanced Catalyst Weaver and duplication setups, so many factories benefit from a dedicated tank for it.
