## How to make Cryofluid

Cryofluid is generated in the [Cryo Chamber](/docs/ascendant_technology/Wiki/Machines/cryo_chamber).
The machine converts **Water** into **Cryofluid** using a Titanium-based catalyst plus **Lapis Lazuli**.

- **1 Titanium + 8 Lapis Lazuli + 1000 mB Water → 800 mB Cryofluid**
- **1 Raw Titanium + 8 Lapis Lazuli + 1000 mB Water → 1600 mB Cryofluid**

That makes **Raw Titanium** the more efficient catalyst if your goal is to fill tanks quickly.

---

## What Cryofluid is used for

### Stabilization and cooling

Cryofluid powers the Cryo Chamber's stabilization side, including:

- stabilizing **Charged Darloonite Crystal**
- de-energizing **Energized Iron** forms
- cooling a **Refined Aetherium Shard** back into a normal **Aetherium Shard**

This makes Cryofluid one of the key bridge resources between volatile processing and safe material handling.

### Steam production

In the [Vaporworks Processor](/docs/ascendant_technology/Wiki/Machines/vaporworks_processor), **1000 mB Cryofluid → 1500 mB Steam**.

That is a better Steam yield than feeding Water directly, which makes Cryofluid a powerful support resource for advanced automation.

### Superior machine support

The **Superior Seed Synth** consumes Cryofluid as an active process cost. Its operating profile changes how heavily it leans on the fluid, so serious seed automation naturally pushes you toward dedicated Cryofluid production.

---

## Storage and transport

Cryofluid can be carried in capsule items, including multiple filled levels and an infinite capsule variant for extreme late-game logistics.

---

## Practical notes

- Cryofluid is a **support fluid**, not an end product.
- If your factory needs **Steam** in bulk, Cryofluid can be worth making even before you fully lean into stabilization recipes.
- **Raw Titanium** is the premium catalyst choice for dedicated Cryofluid generation.
